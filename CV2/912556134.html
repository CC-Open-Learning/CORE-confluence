CORE Framework : Fine-Tuning Player & Camera

Created by Amila Abeygunasekara, last modified on Jan 15, 2024

Player Settings

You can adjust parameters related to player movement using the Nav Mesh Agent component attached to the Player GameObject.

Additionally, Unity’s Navigation window will offer more options to fine-tune its AI Navigation system.

You can get this window by heading into Window → AI → Navigation in Unity’s context menu.

Camera Settings

You will find a ton of settings in the Cinemachine components of the POV Camera GameObject. I will not overwhelm you by going through each of them. I will only point out the settings that usually matter the most.

The settings we should focus on are in the Aim section of the CinemachineVirtualCamera component of the POV Camera GameObject.

All the settings we are going to discuss will be included in the Aim section.

Adjusting Mouse Sensitivity

Mouse sensitivity can be adjusted by editing the Speed values in the Vertical Axis and Horizontal Axis sections.

Additionally, you can tune the acceleration and deceleration effects by tinkering with Accel Time and Decel Time values.

Clipping/Limiting Rotation Angles

You can limit camera rotation/orbit angles by editing Value Range values under the Vertical Axis and Horizontal Axis sections.

Time to Start Looking At a Waypoint or a Given Target

The wait time and look-at time can be adjusted by editing the Wait Time and Recentering Time values in Vertical Recentering and Horizontal Recentering sections.
