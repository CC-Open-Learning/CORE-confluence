CORE Framework : Digital Learning Simulation KPIs and Metrics

Created by Richard Colbourne on May 15, 2024

User Engagement

  • Active Users (number of unique users engaging with the sim)

  • Session Duration (length of time a user spends on each session – likely use an average)

  • Activity Duration (length of time a user spends completing a specific module or section of the sim, i.e. How long a user takes to complete Lab 1 in MPC)

  • Usage Frequency (how many times/how often users use the simulation – could be overall or by user)

  • Interaction Rate (frequency of user interactions (clicks, selections, etc.) inside the sim)

  • Completion Rate (percentage of users who complete the sim – could be broken down to specific modules or sections as well, i.e. How many users completed Lab 1 vs Lab 5 in MPC)

  • Repeat Visit Rate (number of users who use the simulation multiple times – could measure how many times on average a user uses the sim or a percentage of how many users use the sim more than once)

  • Drop-Off Points (places in the sim where users often quit – could be used to discover potential blocks or confusing areas of the sim)

 

Learning Performance Metrics

  • Completion Rate (percentage of users who complete the sim – could be broken down to specific modules or sections as well, i.e. How many users completed Lab 1 vs Lab 5 in MPC)

  • Progress Tracking (tracking user progress through the modules or sections of the sim at an expected speed)

  • Error Rates (frequency and type of mistakes users commonly make while using the sim to identify areas that could give further detail or support to the users through the sim)

 

User Behavioral Metrics

  • Interaction Rate (frequency of user interactions (clicks, selections etc.) inside the sim)

  • Path Analysis (common sequences of decisions, actions, interactions users take in the sim, i.e. do users generally build circuits using similar steps in MPC, allowing us identify and address any paths that may cause confusion or leverage popular paths for additional modules)

  • Tool Usage (tracking how often users use the settings menus or help functions in a sim)

  • Time on Task (length of time a user spends completing a specific task, i.e. a step in an MPC lab) to identify tasks that may be challenging for users)

  • Idle Time (times when users are using the sim, but are inactive to identify if there are parts of the sim that are less engaging)

 

Technical Performance Metrics

  • Load Time (time it takes for the sim to load, could be used to optimize the sims during iterations/updates)

  • Responsiveness (time it takes for the sim to react to a user action, could be used to reduce lag/user frustration with poor performance)

  • Error Rates (how often bugs or crashes occur, sims may need updating to have better exception handling and a way to report a crash)

  • Resource Usage (CPU, memory etc. that the sim uses, use for optimization)

 

ROI and Impact Metrics

  • User Satisfaction (user feedback on satisfaction with sim, would likely need to build a way to solicit this information, i.e. a satisfaction survey at the end of a sim)

  • Behavioral Change (changes in user behavior and practices that can be observed after using a sim, would likely be based on instructor feedback, i.e. learners on average have a better understanding of the sim topic)

  • Organizational Impact (does the sim have an impact on overall organizational metrics, i.e. learner/instructor satisfaction, learner performance)

  • Reduction in Training Costs (any savings related to using the sim vs traditional learning, i.e. number of fuses saved, litres of gas saved, reduction in printed materials etc. that we can gather from our sims).
